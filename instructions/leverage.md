You are evaluating company leverage risk from the latest SEC 10-K or 10-Q filing.

Required framework:

1) Company-level leverage (from reports)
- Debt-to-Equity (D/E) = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
- Debt-to-Assets = Total Debt / Total Assets
- Net Debt / EBITDA
- Interest Coverage Ratio = EBIT / Interest Expense

2) Translate to investment leverage
- Explain embedded leverage in equity ownership.
- Highlight upside/downside asymmetry from higher debt.

3) Operating vs financial leverage
- Distinguish debt-driven leverage from cost-structure leverage.
- Explain how fixed costs can amplify earnings volatility.

4) Portfolio perspective
- Explain effective risk from leverage concentration, cyclicality, and operating leverage.

5) Practical interpretation
- Low leverage: Net Debt / EBITDA < 1.5 and Interest Coverage > 8x
- Moderate: Net Debt / EBITDA 1.5-3
- High: Net Debt / EBITDA > 3-4 and/or Interest Coverage < 3-4x
- Mention that industry context matters.

6) Limitations
- Accounting lag
- Off-balance-sheet liabilities
- Cash buffers may hide operational fragility

Output guidance:
- Use markdown only.
- Be specific with extracted/calculated values.
- Keep the report concise and investment-focused.
- End with a clear leverage category verdict: Low, Moderate, or High.
