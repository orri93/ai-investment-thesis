# Quarterly Investment Thesis Revalidation

These instructions define a **disciplined, repeatable process** for reviewing existing investments after quarterly earnings. The objective is **not** to predict prices or react to short-term noise, but to test whether each investment thesis remains valid.

## Purpose of a quarterly revalidation

A quarterly thesis review is a **light but structured checkpoint**.

Its goals are to:

-	Detect early warning signs
-	Confirm that execution remains broadly consistent with the thesis
-	Identify risks that are moving from theoretical to real
-	Decide whether any position sizing action is warranted

It is not a full re-underwriting of the investment.

## Preparation (before reading earnings)

Re-read the original thesis sections:

-	Core thesis
-	What must go right
-	Key risks
-	Invalidation criteria

Explicitly ask:

-	What evidence would strengthen this thesis?
-	What evidence would weaken or break it?

This step prevents anchoring on management narratives or market reactions.

## What to review in the earnings report

Focus only on items that map directly to the thesis.

Examples:

-	Execution progress on core initiatives
-	Margin trends tied to the business model
-	Capital allocation decisions
-	Balance sheet strength and liquidity
-	Regulatory, competitive, or operational developments

Ignore:

-	Headline EPS beats/misses
-	Short-term guidance language
-	Stock price reactions
-	Media commentary

## Map evidence to the thesis

For each major thesis pillar, assign a simple status:

-	Green → On track or improving
-	Yellow → Mixed, delayed, or uncertain
-	Red → Deteriorating or invalidated

Example structure:

-	Core thesis execution: Green / Yellow / Red
-	Key risk realization: Green / Yellow / Red
-	Capital allocation discipline: Green / Yellow / Red
-	Balance sheet health: Green / Yellow / Red

This forces clarity without false precision.

## Invalidation check (critical step)

Explicitly confirm:

- Did any invalidation criterion trigger this quarter?

If **yes**, the default action is **exit or material reduction**, unless there is clear, evidence-based justification otherwise.

Do not add new assumptions to rescue a broken thesis.

## Allowed decisions

Each quarterly review must end with one of the following decisions:

1. **Add**: Thesis intact and risk/reward improved
2. **Hold**: Thesis intact, no position change
3. **Trim**: Thesis intact, but risk increased or position oversized
4. **Exit**: Thesis broken or capital better deployed elsewhere

Indecision usually signals an unclear thesis.

## One-paragraph conclusion (recommended)

Write a short summary capturing:

-	What changed this quarter
-	What did not change
-	The resulting action (Add / Hold / Trim / Exit)

Example:

"*Margins improved modestly, execution remains on track, and balance sheet strengthened. No invalidation triggers. Thesis intact; hold position size.*"

This creates a **decision log** for future reference.

## Key rules to follow

-	Do not re-write the thesis to fit new facts
-	Do not act on price alone
-	Do not confuse volatility with risk
-	Let evidence test assumptions, not the other way around

## One-sentence rule

*Quarterly revalidation is a test of assumptions, not a reaction to headlines.*
